Colors-demystifying the number of colors

Ever been to a paint store and seen the dispensing machine?

It has 14 cartridges loaded with pigments. These 14 pigments combine with various base paints to produce thousands of shades. We consult qualified color experts to create a vast database of shades.

We also use spectral scanners like the Nix Scanner to match any given color to our database. Essentially, understanding that 14 basic colors in various quantities and permutations can lead to over 16,000 shades is not rocket science. The pigments in the canisters can be natural or organic and are finely crushed for easy dispersion.

Natural pigments generally dont fade easily. Additives such as UV absorbers or stabilizers are included to prevent fading from UV exposure over time to endure the elements

We believe in offering you a wide range of colors to set your own trends. Recommending trending colors is what everyone does, but we know your choices are what truly matter.

Each base can mix with 14 colors, each in 10 increments.

With 5 bases, the total combinations are 510145 \times 10^{14}51014.


This concise summary captures the essence of the total number of possible combinations when considering the mixing of bases and colors as described.

In addition to the calculation of possible combinations, it's worth noting that deriving colors in this context, using 10 discrete increments per color, is straightforward and allows for precise customization of hues. This simplicity facilitates the creation of a wide range of color variations by adjusting the percentage of each color mixed with the base.
